# ADR-035: Pinned-sources manifest for reproducible citation provenance

**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 2026-07-22
**Authors:** Architect agent, with maintainer review
**Implements:** [ADR-031](031-authoring-time-agents-and-skills.md) D4 — materializes the STABLE-snapshot half D4 left abstract.

---

## Context

[ADR-031](031-authoring-time-agents-and-skills.md) D4 split framework-knowledge freshness by volatility: **STABLE canonical terms are pinned to dated snapshots**, VOLATILE specifics are live-verified. It named the STABLE half but did not materialize it — there is no artifact in the repo that records *which dated edition* a stable term was grounded against.

The `classical-lexicon` skill ([ADR-031](031-authoring-time-agents-and-skills.md) D2/D3) is the first surface to feel the gap. `references/lexicon.md` opens by calling itself "a stable snapshot (ADR-031 D4)" but records **no snapshot date and no exact edition/revision per term**, and several of its `Source` cells are free text that cannot be resolved to a document: `classical asset-management / IAM`, `threat-modeling canon; NIST`, `CISA / ENISA guidance`. The lexicon claims the D4 posture without the data that would make it true.

The gap is closed by pinning through a **tracked repo artifact** — a sources manifest at `risk-map/yaml/sources.yaml` — that the lexicon and other content reference by `id`. A per-skill or per-user snapshot embedded in the skill drifts copy-by-copy, the exact failure a single tracked artifact prevents; that alternative is rejected (see Alternatives Considered).

Three coupled questions make this an architecture decision rather than a skill edit:

1. **A new schema'd corpus artifact.** A tracked `risk-map/yaml/*.yaml` with a companion `risk-map/schemas/*.schema.json` and schema-validation wiring is a schema-change / new-surface trigger. It also sits adjacent to [ADR-034](034-corpus-change-landing-sequence.md), which orders how corpus files land; whether `sources.yaml` is governed by that sequence or exempt must be settled rather than assumed (D5b).
2. **Two existing pinning surfaces already occupy adjacent ground.** [ADR-027](027-framework-versioning-and-mapping-convention.md) pins *framework editions* in `frameworks.yaml` (`version` / `versionId` / `priorVersions`, per-edition controlled-vocabulary enums, `supersedes` lineage) and is the version-pinning precedent to mirror. [ADR-016](016-reference-strategy.md) pins *ad-hoc citations* in per-entry `externalReferences[]` (typed, id-referenced, `https://`-only). A sources manifest sits between them, and unless its boundary against both is drawn explicitly, the corpus grows a second citation registry that drifts against the first — the failure ADR-027 exists to prevent, reintroduced on a new axis.
3. **Scope is unfixed.** A manifest that serves only the lexicon and a corpus-wide citation registry are different artifacts with different curation costs. Deciding scope up front prevents a second migration.

This ADR decides the architecture only. It authors no schema, no manifest data, and no lexicon edit; those are the downstream implementing PR.

## Decision

We adopt a **tracked, schema-validated pinned-sources manifest** at `risk-map/yaml/sources.yaml`, governed by a new `risk-map/schemas/sources.schema.json`, as the corpus-wide registry of citable source editions. Content and skills cite an entry by a stable `id`; the entry pins the exact edition/revision, its publication date, and the date CoSAI verified the pin. The manifest is a **provenance sibling** to `frameworks.yaml`, not a subsumption of it or a cross-referenced dependency of it, and it is distinct from ADR-016 `externalReferences`. A source that cannot be fully pinned is **kept and flagged, never dropped**.

### D1. Scope — a corpus-wide pinned-sources registry, not a lexicon-only manifest

`sources.yaml` is the **corpus-wide registry of pinned citable sources**, not a private lexicon appendix. Any skill or content surface that needs to cite a dated source edition references a `sources.yaml` entry by `id`; the `classical-lexicon` is the first consumer, not the only sanctioned one.

The decisive reason is drift avoidance. NIST SP 800-162, Saltzer & Schroeder 1975, and RFC 9334 are cited by the lexicon today and are plausibly cited again by a risk, a control, or the `framework-mappings` style guide tomorrow. A lexicon-scoped manifest would force each new consumer to either re-pin the same document (two entries for one edition, free to disagree on date or URL) or reach across a private skill directory for a citation surface — the same fan-out defect [ADR-031](031-authoring-time-agents-and-skills.md) D2 rejected for per-framework reference packs and [ADR-016](016-reference-strategy.md) D3 rejected for duplicated schema definitions. One registry, one pin per edition, referenced by id, is the established repo pattern for shared reference data.

Scope is deliberately bounded on one axis to keep curation load honest: `sources.yaml` holds **document editions cited as provenance** (standards, papers, RFCs, guidance publications). It is **not** the home for the volatile per-technique identifiers ADR-027 governs (those stay in `frameworks.yaml` and its mappings, D2), nor for the ad-hoc per-entry web citations ADR-016 governs (those stay in `externalReferences`, D3). Corpus-wide in *reach* (any consumer may cite it), narrow in *content type* (pinned source editions only).

### D2. Boundary against `frameworks.yaml` / ADR-027 — disjoint-by-role siblings

`sources.yaml` and `frameworks.yaml` are **sibling registries partitioned by role**, living side by side under `risk-map/yaml/`. Neither subsumes the other, and a cross-reference between them is **permitted but not required** (D2a). The partition is:

- **`frameworks.yaml` holds mapping *targets*.** A framework entry exists so that `risks`/`controls`/`components`/`personas` can *cross-walk* their content into an external taxonomy via the `mappings` object (`mappings.mitre-atlas: [AML.T0043@5.0.1]`). Its version machinery (ADR-027 `version`/`versionId`/`priorVersions`, per-edition enums, per-value pinning) exists to keep those *mappings* integrity-checkable as the taxonomy re-versions. A framework is a live cross-reference system whose identifiers are validated against edition-specific value patterns.
- **`sources.yaml` holds citation *provenance*.** A source entry exists so that a skill or content surface can *cite* the dated document it grounded a term or claim against (`nist-sp-800-162`, `saltzer-schroeder-1975`, `rfc-9334`). It answers "which exact edition backs this?" — a provenance question, not a cross-walk. A source is a pinned document snapshot, not a taxonomy whose members are enumerated and mapped.

This mirrors a partition the repo already lives with: [ADR-016](016-reference-strategy.md) D3 kept `externalReferences` (ad-hoc citations) deliberately separate from `mappings` (framework cross-walks) — "Existing `mappings` … remains the surface for bulk taxonomy-to-taxonomy mapping; `externalReferences` is for ad-hoc citations." `sources.yaml` extends the *citation* side of that already-drawn line to shared, dated, reusable source editions; it does not blur the *mapping* side.

We **reject subsuming `frameworks.yaml` into `sources.yaml`** (or the reverse). A framework carries `applicableTo`, per-value mapping patterns, and the ADR-027 drift machinery that a plain citation does not need and must not inherit; a source carries an `edition`/`snapshotDate` pin that a mapping target does not use. Merging them would force every entry to carry the union of both field sets, most of it inapplicable, and would couple the citation-curation cadence to the ADR-027 version-change process (D10) that governs mapping integrity — two cadences that should stay independent.

#### D2a. The overlap case — NIST appears in both, and that is correct

The load-bearing worry is that "NIST" lives in both files. It does, and the partition resolves it cleanly because the two NIST presences are **different objects at different granularities**:

- `frameworks.yaml` has `nist-ai-rmf` — the **AI RMF taxonomy** as a *mapping target*, whose members (`GOVERN-6.2@1.0`) controls cross-walk into.
- `sources.yaml` would have `nist-sp-800-162` — a **specific NIST publication** (the ABAC / policy-point guide) cited as the *provenance* for the PEP/PDP vocabulary.

These are not the same entity re-registered; they are a taxonomy versus a document, and no consistency obligation binds them because they carry different data for different purposes. A source entry **may** optionally record that it belongs to the same publisher family as a framework via a free-text `publisher` field, but there is **no required `frameworkId` cross-link and no obligation that a NIST framework and a NIST source agree on anything** — they pin different things (a taxonomy edition versus a document edition). Keeping the link optional avoids manufacturing a consistency invariant that would itself need a validator and could drift.

**A framework's own spec document is cited through the framework entry, never re-pinned in `sources.yaml`.** A framework already carries a `documentUri` (the direct URI to its spec) and a `lastUpdated` date — `nist-ai-rmf` (`NIST.AI.100-1.pdf`, `2023-01-26`), `iso-22989`, and `eu-ai-act` each carry both. Citing that spec *as a document* (e.g. pointing at the AI RMF PDF itself, not cross-walking content into the AI RMF taxonomy) resolves to the framework entry's `documentUri`; it is **not** duplicated into a `sources.yaml` entry. Re-pinning it would register the same publication in two places — `frameworks.yaml` (`documentUri` + `lastUpdated`) and `sources.yaml` (`url` + `date`) — with two dates free to diverge and no validator reconciling them, exactly the two-registry drift D2 exists to prevent. `sources.yaml` therefore pins only publications that are **not** themselves framework mapping targets: a specific NIST SP (SP 800-162, SP 800-207) that has no `frameworks.yaml` entry, a paper (Saltzer & Schroeder 1975), an RFC (9334). The test a contributor holding a single PDF URL applies: **if that URL is already some framework's `documentUri`, cite the framework entry; otherwise pin it in `sources.yaml`.**

**Where each citation goes (the explicit routing rule):**

| Citation is… | Goes in | Referenced by |
|---|---|---|
| A cross-walk of CoSAI content into an external taxonomy's identifiers | `frameworks.yaml` `mappings` (ADR-027) | `mappings.<framework>: [<pinned-value>]` |
| A framework's own spec document, cited *as a document* (its URL is already a framework's `documentUri`) | `frameworks.yaml` `documentUri` (ADR-027) | the framework entry (no `sources.yaml` re-pin) |
| A dated source edition grounding a term/claim, reused across surfaces, that is **not** a framework's own spec | **`sources.yaml` (this ADR)** | **a `sources.yaml` `id`** |
| An ad-hoc, per-entry web citation (paper, CWE, advisory, editorial pointer) local to one entry's prose | `externalReferences` (ADR-016) | `{{ref:identifier}}` sentinel |

### D3. Manifest shape and the reference mechanism

A `sources.yaml` entry mirrors the ADR-027 registry discipline (an `id` distinct from the human title, an explicit edition designator, a verified-pin date) and the ADR-016 `externalReferences` id discipline (stable author-chosen `id`, `https://`-only `url`). The field set the schema (D4) will realize:

- **`id`** — required. The stable token content/skills cite. Lowercase-kebab, `^[a-z0-9-]+$` (D4). This is the value the lexicon's `Source (sources.yaml id)` column carries, and the same indirection ADR-027 uses (the reference holds an id; the registry holds the detail).
- **`title`** — required. The document's title, for display and disambiguation.
- **`publisher`** — required. The issuing body (`NIST`, `ISO/IEC`, `IETF`, `OASIS`, author name for a paper). Free text; it does **not** cross-link to `frameworks.yaml` (D2a).
- **`edition`** — required. The exact edition/revision designation (`SP 800-162`, `27002:2022`, `RFC 9334`) — the "exact edition/revision per term" the defect calls for.
- **`revision`** — optional. A finer revision where the edition alone is ambiguous (`Rev. 1`).
- **`date`** — required. Publication date of the pinned edition (`YYYY-MM-DD`), distinct from when CoSAI verified it.
- **`snapshotDate`** — required. The date CoSAI pinned/verified this entry — the D4 "dated snapshot" itself. Re-verifying a stable source updates `snapshotDate` deliberately, the ADR-027 re-pin discipline applied to sources.
- **`url`** — required. `https://`-only (ADR-016 D3 constraint), pointing at the pinned edition.
- **`section`** — optional. A section/clause locator where practical (`3.2`).

**How content references an entry.** The reference mechanism is **by `id`**, resolved at the citing surface's own layer. For the lexicon, the free-text `Source` column becomes a `Source (sources.yaml id)` column carrying the `id`; the skill may resolve the id to a full citation for the reader at output time, but the *stored* value is the id. This is the D2/ADR-016 indirection: the citation surface holds a short stable token, the manifest holds the pinned detail, and a rename or re-pin happens in one place.

#### D3a. The keep-and-flag fallback for an un-pinnable source

A source that cannot be fully pinned to a traceable dated edition — the lexicon's `classical asset-management / IAM`, `threat-modeling canon; NIST`, `CISA / ENISA guidance` are the live cases — is **kept and flagged, never dropped and never silently downgraded**. This is the `Status: Draft`-analog for a source: presence is retained, incompleteness is marked, and a maintainer resolves it later.

The flag lives on the **citing surface, not the manifest**. The manifest holds only fully-pinned entries (every required field present, which the schema enforces — D4); a source that cannot yet meet that bar simply has **no manifest entry**, and the citing surface records a `missing-pin` maintainer flag in its own flag column instead of a `sources.yaml` id. This keeps the schema strict (no half-pinned entries polluting the registry) while honoring keep-and-flag (the citation is retained on the surface, visibly marked for backfill). When the source is later pinned, a manifest entry is added and the surface swaps its `missing-pin` flag for the new id. `missing-pin` is one token in the classical-lexicon skill's controlled maintainer-flag vocabulary, greppable for the backfill sweep.

### D4. Schema and validation posture

A new schema `risk-map/schemas/sources.schema.json` governs the manifest, modeled on the existing `risk-map/yaml/*` schema conventions (the same pattern `frameworks.schema.json` and the shared `external-references.schema.json` follow). The ADR fixes the *constraints*; the implementing PR writes the file.

- **Closed field set.** `additionalProperties: false` on the source object, matching `frameworks.schema.json`'s framework object. A stray or misspelled field fails validation rather than passing silently — the same posture ADR-027 and ADR-016 take.
- **Required vs optional.** Required: `id`, `title`, `publisher`, `edition`, `date`, `snapshotDate`, `url`. Optional: `revision`, `section`. The schema enforcing all seven required fields is exactly what makes the D3a "no half-pinned entries" property hold.
- **`id` format.** `^[a-z0-9-]+$`, matching the `versionId` charset discipline (ADR-027 D2b) and the lowercase-kebab external-reference id convention (ADR-016 D3). `id` uniqueness across the manifest is enforced (a duplicate id is a registry error), mirroring ADR-027's uniqueness assertion on `versionId`.
- **Date fields.** `date` and `snapshotDate` constrained to `^\d{4}-\d{2}-\d{2}$`, the `lastUpdated` pattern `frameworks.schema.json` already uses. Both authored quoted to avoid YAML date-coercion, the hygiene note `frameworks.yaml` already carries.
- **`url` scheme.** `https://`-only, the ADR-016 D3 constraint (HTTP rejected).

**Validation wiring — two tiers, one deferred:**

- **Schema validation (ships with the manifest, in one unit).** `sources.yaml` is registered against `sources.schema.json` in the same `check-jsonschema` pre-commit path that validates the other `risk-map/yaml/*` files, and in `validate-all.sh` for CI parity (the parity gap ADR-027's 2026-06-10 addendum closed for the consumer schemas). This is a strict, self-contained check with no external catalog. The schema and the seed manifest land together as a single infrastructure unit on `main` (D5a) — the "no schema without its data" co-bundling property [ADR-034](034-corpus-change-landing-sequence.md) enforces for corpus entities, satisfied here without ADR-034's develop-side sequence applying (D5b).
- **Reference-integrity check (ships with the first consumer that uses ids — the lexicon rewrite).** A check that every `sources.yaml` id *cited by a consumer* resolves to a manifest entry lands **with the D5 step-3 lexicon rewrite**, not deferred to a hypothetical later YAML-native consumer. The reasoning: the lexicon rewrite is *itself* the first surface to carry `sources.yaml` ids, so it is the first moment a dangling-id hole can exist — and shipping the rewrite without the resolver would introduce exactly the invisible-typo failure ADR-016 D6's linter closed for prose references, on the sources axis (a mistyped id *looks* pinned, so it gets no `missing-pin` flag and nothing catches it — see Negative consequences). Deferring the resolver "until a YAML-native consumer appears" would leave that hole open for the entire interval the lexicon is the sole consumer, which is the near-term steady state.

  The check is a Markdown-column id-resolver: extract the `id` from each `Source (sources.yaml id)` cell (and each `missing-pin` flag), and assert every non-flag id resolves against `sources.yaml`'s id set. This is **not materially harder than the ADR-016 D6 prose-reference linter that already exists** — D6 already reads identifier enums from live schemas and `externalReferences[].id` lists from YAML, tokenizes Markdown/prose fields, and resolves `{{ref:identifier}}` sentinels against a per-entry id set (`016-reference-strategy.md` D6, steps 2–3). The sources resolver reuses the same two moves against a simpler target: one flat global id set from one YAML file, resolved from one fixed Markdown column, with no per-entry scoping and no sentinel grammar to parse. The "consumer is Markdown, not YAML" objection is not a blocker — ADR-016 D6 is *also* a Markdown/prose resolver; the only YAML in either is the source-of-truth id list, which `sources.yaml` supplies directly. The check flags both directions D3a leaves uncaught by review alone: an id on the surface that resolves to no manifest entry (dangling id), and — as a warn-level companion — a manifest entry no surface cites (orphaned entry). This keeps ADR-027's honest-tiering spirit (ship the check when its consumer exists) while refusing to ship the consumer without it.

**Lineage fields are deferred, not adopted.** ADR-027 carries `supersedes`/`priorVersions` for framework re-versioning. Sources re-version far more slowly than mapping taxonomies, and the `snapshotDate` re-pin already records deliberate re-verification. We therefore **do not adopt lineage fields at this ADR**; `snapshotDate` re-pinning is the near-term mechanism. Adding `supersedes`/`priorVersions` later — if a citation set turns out to need multi-edition coexistence the way mappings do — is a future amendment to this ADR, not implementation latitude.

### D5. Sequencing and definition of done

#### D5a. Branch boundary — `sources.yaml` bases on `main` as infrastructure

`sources.yaml` and its schema base on **`main`**, as infrastructure, not on `develop` as content. The file lives under `risk-map/yaml/` for locality with the other schema'd YAML, but its *role* is a provenance registry consumed by skills, and skills are infrastructure on `main` ([ADR-031](031-authoring-time-agents-and-skills.md) D5: tooling on `main`, content on `develop`). Its first and near-term-only consumer — the `classical-lexicon` skill — is itself on `main`, so the manifest, its schema, and its consumer all sit on one branch.

This choice is deliberate specifically to **avoid inheriting the [ADR-027](027-framework-versioning-and-mapping-convention.md) D7 M1 cross-branch ordering problem.** That invariant governs a strict schema pattern on `main` validating content values on `develop`: the two land non-atomically, so the schema must hold a loose catch-all until the content bridges develop→main before flipping strict. Basing `sources.yaml` on `main` collapses that gap — schema, manifest, and the citing lexicon co-reside on `main`, so the schema-then-seed handoff (steps 2–3 below) never straddles a branch boundary and there is no develop→main bridge to sequence around.

The counter-pull is real and named here: `sources.yaml` sits beside `risks.yaml` / `controls.yaml`, which are content on `develop`, so a reader may expect it to follow content branching. It does not — placement follows *role*, not directory. If a future YAML-native consumer on `develop` (a risk or control field carrying a `sources.yaml` id) is added, that consumer's reference-integrity check would then span a `main` manifest and `develop` content, re-opening exactly the ADR-027 D7 M1 develop→main ordering question; at that point the manifest keeps its schema strict on `main` while the develop-side reference check holds warn-only until the citing content bridges to `main`, mirroring D7 M1's catch-all-until-bridged posture. This branch call lands with the ADR as Draft and is confirmed at acceptance.

#### D5b. Relationship to ADR-034 (corpus landing sequence)

[ADR-034](034-corpus-change-landing-sequence.md) orders how corpus entities (`components`/`personas`/`controls`/`risks`) land relative to one another, keyed to the corpus *reference graph*, and bundles a new entity's `risk-map/schemas/**` id-enum edit with its `risk-map/yaml/**` entry as one content unit on `develop`. **`sources.yaml`+schema landing is exempt from the ADR-034 sequence**, on two grounds. First, `sources.yaml` is not a node in the corpus reference graph — nothing in `components`/`personas`/`controls`/`risks` references it, and it references none of them — so it has no position in ADR-034's topological layering; it is a provenance sibling, not a corpus entity. Second, per D5a it bases on `main` as infrastructure, whereas ADR-034 governs content landing on `develop`. The schema-then-seed handoff below is therefore sequenced by *this* ADR (D5, steps 1–3), not by ADR-034's layers; ADR-034's schema+YAML co-bundling rule does not bind here because the sources schema and its manifest already co-reside on `main` and land together as a single infrastructure unit (step 2), which satisfies the same "no schema without its data" property ADR-034 enforces for corpus entities.

#### D5c. Landing order for this manifest

The manifest gates only the lexicon's shift from free-text `Source` cells to manifest `id` references; other lexicon and skill work does not depend on it (the source column can carry free-text citations until the manifest lands). The dependency order:

1. **This ADR, accepted.** The boundary (D2), scope (D1), shape (D3), and validation posture (D4) are fixed. Lands `Status: Draft`; maintainer flips to `Accepted`.
2. **Schema + seed manifest.** `risk-map/schemas/sources.schema.json` (D4) and an initial `risk-map/yaml/sources.yaml` seeded with the sources the *asserted* lexicon terms cite (NIST SP 800-162 / 800-207 / 800-53, Saltzer & Schroeder 1975, Anderson 1972, RFC 9334, the OWASP and CWE entries the asserted terms rest on), each fully pinned (`edition`/`date`/`snapshotDate`). The schema is code-adjacent and routes TDD (`testing` → `code-reviewer` → `swe` → `code-reviewer`); the seed manifest is content that routes through the `content-reviewer`.
3. **Lexicon rewrite, with the id-resolver.** The lexicon's free-text `Source` column becomes `Source (sources.yaml id)` carrying manifest ids; the un-pinnable citations (`classical asset-management / IAM`, `threat-modeling canon; NIST`, `CISA / ENISA guidance`) get a `missing-pin` flag (D3a) rather than a fabricated entry; the vague "stable snapshot" line at the top of `lexicon.md` is replaced by the concrete snapshot the manifest now records. The **Markdown-column reference-integrity resolver (D4)** lands in this same step, because this rewrite is the first surface to carry `sources.yaml` ids and therefore the first place a dangling id could pass unflagged. The resolver routes TDD (`testing` → `code-reviewer` → `swe` → `code-reviewer`) like any pre-commit hook; the column rewrite routes through the `content-reviewer`.

**Definition of done for this ADR:** the schema constraints, the frameworks/ADR-027 boundary, the scope, the shape, the branch boundary (D5a), the ADR-034 relationship (D5b), and the keep-and-flag fallback are decided and defensible; the schema *file*, the manifest *data*, the lexicon *edit*, and the reference-integrity resolver *implementation* are explicitly out of scope and belong to steps 2–3.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Lexicon-only manifest (bundled under the skill).** A `sources` file living inside `scripts/skills/classical-lexicon/`. Rejected (D1): it re-pins shared documents per consumer and drifts copy-by-copy — the fan-out defect ADR-031 D2 and ADR-016 D3 both reject — and does not deliver corpus-wide consistency. Corpus-wide reach, narrow content type (D1), is the shape that avoids the second migration.
- **Subsume `frameworks.yaml` into `sources.yaml` (one citation registry).** Rejected (D2): a framework is a mapping *target* with `applicableTo`, per-value patterns, and the ADR-027 drift machinery; a source is a citation *provenance* pin. Merging forces every entry to carry the union of both field sets and couples the citation cadence to the ADR-027 version-change process — two cadences that must stay independent.
- **Make `sources.yaml` reference `frameworks.yaml` by required id.** A required `frameworkId` on each source, cross-linking NIST-the-source to NIST-the-framework. Rejected (D2a): the two NIST presences are different objects at different granularities (a document versus a taxonomy) with no consistency obligation between them; a required cross-link manufactures an invariant that itself needs a validator and can drift. The link is optional at most.
- **Extend ADR-016 `externalReferences` to carry the pin instead of a new manifest.** Reuse the per-entry citation array for source pinning. Rejected: `externalReferences` is deliberately *per-entry and ad-hoc* (ADR-016 D3) — a shared, dated source edition cited from many surfaces would be duplicated into every entry that cites it, re-introducing the drift D1 avoids. `sources.yaml` is the shared-and-dated tier; `externalReferences` stays the ad-hoc per-entry tier (D2 routing table).
- **Adopt ADR-027 `supersedes`/`priorVersions` lineage now.** Rejected for this ADR (D4): sources re-version slowly and `snapshotDate` records deliberate re-verification; lineage fields are machinery ahead of need. Left as a future amendment rather than adopted speculatively.
- **Defer the id↔manifest reference-integrity validator until a YAML-native consumer appears.** Rejected (D4): the lexicon rewrite is *itself* the first surface to carry `sources.yaml` ids, so deferring the resolver would leave a dangling-id hole open (a mistyped id looks pinned, gets no `missing-pin` flag, and nothing catches it — the ADR-016 D6 invisible-typo failure on the sources axis) for the entire near-term interval the lexicon is the sole consumer. The "consumer is Markdown, not YAML" objection does not hold: ADR-016 D6 is itself a Markdown/prose resolver, and the sources resolver is a strictly simpler case (one flat global id set, one fixed column, no per-entry scoping, no sentinel grammar). The resolver therefore ships with the D5 step-3 lexicon rewrite, not deferred.

## Consequences

**Positive**

- **ADR-031 D4's STABLE-snapshot half is materialized.** The abstract "pin stable terms to dated snapshots" becomes a concrete tracked artifact with an `edition` and a `snapshotDate` per source. The lexicon's D4 claim becomes true rather than asserted.
- **One pin per edition, cited by id, corpus-wide.** Any surface citing NIST SP 800-162 resolves the same entry; a re-pin or a URL fix happens in one place and propagates by reference — the single-source-of-truth property ADR-016 D3 established for `externalReferences`, extended to shared dated sources.
- **The two-registry drift risk is closed by an explicit partition, not left implicit.** `frameworks.yaml` = mapping targets, `sources.yaml` = citation provenance, `externalReferences` = ad-hoc per-entry citations (D2 routing table). The boundary is stated per-surface, so a contributor knows where each citation goes without guessing, and the corpus does not grow two overlapping citation registries.
- **Keep-and-flag keeps the schema strict without losing data.** The manifest holds only fully-pinned entries; un-pinnable citations stay visible on the citing surface with a greppable `missing-pin` flag for backfill (D3a). Strict registry, no silent drops.

**Negative**

- **A new curation surface with its own upkeep.** `sources.yaml` must be seeded and kept current; `snapshotDate` re-verification is a deliberate maintenance action (no auto-refresh, by design). ADR-031's Consequences already named authoring curation load; this concentrates the source-pinning slice of it in one artifact rather than spreading it across skills.
- **The registry can sit sparse in the interim and partially self-defeat the D1 consistency goal.** Because the manifest is seeded from a single Markdown consumer (the lexicon) and the resolver that would make ids first-class only lands with that consumer's rewrite, a second surface wanting to cite a source before its entry exists may cite ad hoc rather than wait for a manifest entry — so the corpus-wide-consistency intent of D1 holds only to the extent surfaces actually route through the manifest during the sparse early period.
- **The dangling-id hole is real between step 2 and step 3, and is closed only when the resolver lands.** A `sources.yaml` id that is mistyped or points at no manifest entry *looks* pinned — it sits in the id column, not the `missing-pin` column — so D3a's keep-and-flag review does **not** flag it: keep-and-flag catches citations the author knows are un-pinnable, not ids the author believes are good but got wrong. This is precisely the invisible-prose-typo failure the ADR-016 D6 linter closed, reintroduced on the sources axis. The manifest side has the mirror hole: a manifest entry no surface cites (an orphan) is equally uncaught by review. The D4 Markdown-column resolver closes both — dangling ids as a block, orphans as a warn — but it ships **with the step-3 lexicon rewrite**, so any interim in which a step-2 seed manifest exists and a surface carries hand-typed ids *before* the resolver lands has the hole open, caught only by review. Sequencing the resolver into step 3 (the first step that produces ids) minimizes that interim to zero for the lexicon; the accepted residual is any *other* surface that starts citing ids ahead of the resolver generalizing to it.
- **Auditing "what a surface cites and how solid each citation is" spans two surfaces until a unified index exists.** After D3a, a surface's citations are split: pinned ones resolve into `sources.yaml` (structured, queryable), un-pinnable ones are a `missing-pin` string in a Markdown flag column (unstructured). Answering "show me every citation on this surface and its pin status" therefore requires reading both the manifest and the flag column and joining them by hand — there is no single index over both until a cross-surface validator or report that enumerates ids-plus-flags lands (itself downstream of the step-3 resolver, which produces the first machine-readable id inventory). This fragmentation is an accepted cost of keeping the schema strict (D3a: no half-pinned entries) rather than admitting partial entries that would unify the surfaces at the cost of a polluted registry.
- **A third citation tier for contributors to learn.** Alongside `mappings` (ADR-027) and `externalReferences` (ADR-016), `sources.yaml` is a third place a citation can live. The D2 routing table is the mitigation, but the taxonomy is genuinely three-way and must be documented for authors.
- **Supply-chain surface, though a light one.** The manifest records outbound `https://` URLs to external documents; it fetches nothing and executes nothing (it is inert YAML validated by `check-jsonschema`), so the pre-commit / provenance axes ([ADR-014](014-yaml-content-security-posture.md)) are narrow. The live axis is that a pinned `url` can rot; `snapshotDate` records when the pin was last verified so staleness is at least visible.

**Follow-up**

- **Schema + seed manifest (D5 step 2).** Author `risk-map/schemas/sources.schema.json` (closed fields, `^[a-z0-9-]+$` id, `https://` url, `YYYY-MM-DD` dates, seven required fields) via TDD; seed `risk-map/yaml/sources.yaml` with the asserted-lexicon-term sources, fully pinned, via the `content-reviewer`. Wire `sources.yaml` → `sources.schema.json` into the `check-jsonschema` pre-commit path and `validate-all.sh`.
- **Lexicon rewrite + id-resolver (D5 step 3).** Swap the free-text `Source` column for `Source (sources.yaml id)`; add `missing-pin` flags for the un-pinnable citations; replace the vague snapshot line with the concrete manifest-recorded snapshot (routes through the `content-reviewer` and the skill neutrality check). Build the Markdown-column id↔manifest reference-integrity resolver in the same step (routes TDD), since this rewrite is the first surface carrying `sources.yaml` ids.
- **Extend the id-resolver to a future YAML-native consumer.** If a `sources.yaml` id is later referenced from a risk/control field on `develop`, generalize the step-3 resolver to that surface and sequence its develop→main reference check per the D5a / ADR-027 D7 M1 catch-all-until-bridged posture. Tracked as backlog against that consumer's arrival.
- **Backfill sweep.** The `missing-pin` citations (and the ADR-031 D3b international-equivalence backfill) are a post-adoption sweep, filed against the backlog rather than blocking the manifest's first landing.
